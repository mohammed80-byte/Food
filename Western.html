Western Food

Western cuisine encompasses a diverse range of cooking traditions originating from European countries, the Americas, and Australasia. Influenced heavily by classic French techniques, many Western meals feature meat, dairy, and wheat products as staples. Roasting, baking, and grilling are common cooking methods.

Typical Western dinners are often structured around a protein, like beef or chicken, with a starchy side dish such as potatoes or pasta, and vegetables. Courses are usually served separately, starting with soups or salads and ending with desserts like pastries or cakes

Unlike some Asian cooking, which emphasizes smaller portions and a high volume of vegetables, Western meals historically focus on larger, meat-centric servings. Sauces are a foundational component, with many modern variations derived from the five French "mother sauces". Fast-food variations like hamburgers and pizza have also become widely known as Western food.

Examples of Western Food

Apple Pie

An apple pie is a pie in which the principal filling is apples. It is often served with whipped cream, ice cream, custard or cheddar cheese. It is generally double-crusted, with pastry both above and below the filling; the upper crust may be solid or latticed

Mac n Cheese

Macaroni and cheese is a pasta dish of macaroni covered in cheese sauce, most commonly cheddar sauce. Its origins trace back to cheese and pasta casseroles in medieval England.

Hamburger

A hamburger consists of fillings—usually a patty of ground meat, typically beef—placed inside a sliced bun or bread roll.
